Kate Bush – Aerial

Celebration of the sea, the sky, the banality and joy of life. Portraying the sublime beauty of the world as well as mundane objects, like a simple washing machine, in a poetic manner that strikes right trough the heart. The song of a blackbird occurs as the leitmotif of the album, smartly depicted as a waveform on the cover. Tons of emotions bursting out of Bush’s dramatic soprano in this 2005 record released after a twelve-year hiatus. An all time favorite that I keep coming back to.

About

In the lost and found is a joint project of Elena Davcheva and Igor Stojanovski. The website is a public archive of our own writings about music as well as a platform for promoting new and exciting bands and artists.

We are mostly focused on independent and underground music. More specifically, we are into psychedelic rock and its derivatives, different forms of ambient and mainly experimental music. Yet, we do not shy away from more exposed music artists as well. No rules apply here and we are not trying to be snobs at all.
